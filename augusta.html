Medicare in Augusta

Coverage that works across the river

Augusta anchors the Central Savannah River Area, with care led by Wellstar MCG Health (the Medical College of Georgia), Doctors Hospital — home to the Joseph M. Still Burn Center, the largest burn facility in the country — and Piedmont Augusta. Because the metro straddles the Georgia–South Carolina state line, plan networks and service areas deserve a careful look if you cross into North Augusta or Aiken for care.

Augusta is also a strong military and veteran community (Fort Gordon). If you have TRICARE For Life or VA benefits, how they coordinate with Medicare changes which plan makes sense — see our Veterans page.

When you can enroll

Turning 65? Your Initial Enrollment Period is the 7 months around your birthday. Already on Medicare? The Annual Election Period is Oct 15–Dec 7, and Medicare Advantage Open Enrollment runs Jan 1–Mar 31.

Questions from Augusta

Frequently asked questions

Which Medicare plans are available in Augusta?

Availability changes by ZIP code, but most Augusta residents can choose among several Medicare Advantage plans, a range of Medicare Supplement (Medigap) policies, and standalone Part D drug plans. We compare what is offered at your address and what fits your doctors and medications.

Will my Wellstar MCG or Doctors Hospital doctors take a Medicare Advantage plan?

It depends on the specific plan's network. Before you enroll, we confirm whether your providers at Wellstar MCG or Doctors Hospital and your prescriptions are covered, so there are no surprises in January.

How much does your help cost?

Nothing. Independent agents are paid by the carriers when you enroll, so comparing plans and reviewing your coverage each year is free to you.
